# A Draft in the Rain

By lunch, the floating library had begun to rain paper.

Pages slid past the window and stuck to the glass, each one carrying a sentence that stopped before it became brave. Yolanda read them out loud until the room filled with beginnings.

One page said the hero was lost.

Another said the map was wrong.

The smallest page said only this:

> I am not finished, but I am ready.

Yolanda smiled, sharpened her pencil, and opened the window.
